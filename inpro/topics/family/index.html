Family Services

ACS programs, CYS childcare and youth, spouse sponsorship, EFMP continuity, family advocacy, and spouse employment — the family-side support stack.

7 entries · across 4 phases

Before You Move 2

  • EFMP: enrollment, continuity, and what actually transfers

    EFMP moves with you, but each installation maintains its own case file. Start the transfer the day orders drop. In Wiesbaden, ACS is your liaison; DoDEA handles school-side IEPs; TRICARE Overseas covers medical.

    Verify first Verified 2026-04-16

  • Spouse employment in Wiesbaden

    NAF and GS jobs on-post are the easiest route. Working on the German economy requires an Arbeitserlaubnis. Remote US work is often simplest. ACS ERP, MSEP, and MyCAA all help.

    Verified 2026-04-16

When You Arrive 2

  • Army Community Service (ACS): Hainerberg

    The one-stop support hub: spouse sponsorship, lending closet, relocation readiness, financial counseling, EFMP liaison, and more. Bldg 7790, Hainerberg.

    Verify first Verified 2026-04-16

  • CYS: child care, SKIES, sports, teens

    Child & Youth Services runs on-post childcare (CDC, SKIES), school-age care, Youth Sports, and Middle School/Teen programs. Subsidized by income. Register via webtrac; book early: popular programs fill in weeks.

    Verify first Verified 2026-04-16

Life in Wiesbaden 1

  • ACS deployment support: pre-deployment, during, and reintegration

    Army Community Service runs deployment programs for the soldier and family: Military Family Life Counselors, Pre-Deployment Readiness briefings, Hearts Apart events for kids, Family Readiness Group liaison, and Post-Deployment reintegration workshops. Free and confidential.

    Verify first Verified 2026-04-20

Sponsors 2

  • How to be a good sponsor

    You were picked because you've been here. Your job isn't to re-welcome them to the Army: it's to answer the stupid questions they're afraid to ask their unit. Reach out 60 days before, meet them at baggage claim if you can, and stay reachable for week one.

    Verified 2026-04-16

  • Working with your sponsor: get the most out of the person assigned to you

    Your sponsor is a peer contact at the gaining unit, not a concierge. Email first, ask specific questions, share arrival details early. Most sponsor relationships underperform because the newcomer waits for the sponsor to drive. You get what you ask for.

    Verified 2026-04-20
