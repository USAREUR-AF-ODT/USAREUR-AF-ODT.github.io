Mental health resources: on-post and off-post

ARHC behavioral health for routine; ATLAS-referral German therapists for longer-term; chaplain for confidential pastoral care; Military Crisis Line (988) 24/7; SHARP for assault response. Don't wait until it's acute.

What

Mental health care in Wiesbaden spans four paths depending on need and urgency:

  1. Urgent / Crisis: Military Crisis Line (988), SHARP, Domestic Violence, on-post ER (for immediate danger)
  2. Routine Army Behavioral Health: ARHC’s behavioral health team; Active Duty primary, Family with capacity
  3. Longer-term therapy / psychiatry: ATLAS-referred German civilian providers
  4. Pastoral counseling (100% confidential): Chaplains; confidentiality privileged

Each path has different access, different hours, and different confidentiality.

Urgent / Crisis (available 24/7)

Suicidal thoughts

  • Call 988 (Veterans / Military Crisis Line)
  • From Germany: 001-844-702-5495
  • Text 988
  • Chat via 988lifeline.org
  • Service is free, confidential, staffed by trained counselors

Domestic violence

  • Hotline (US-staffed, 24/7): +49-162-297-5625
  • On-post Military Police: DSN 548-7777 / +49-611-143-548-7777
  • German Polizei (off-post): 110

Sexual assault

  • SHARP Victim Advocate (Wiesbaden): DSN 53-SHARP (74277) or +49-162-296-6741
  • 24/7 response
  • Confidential reporting options: restricted (no command involvement) or unrestricted (command + investigation)

Immediate medical danger

  • ARHC clinic hours: DSN 590-1300
  • Off-post ER: go to HSK, Asklepios, or St. Josefs
  • German 112: emergency medical dispatch

Routine Army Behavioral Health (ARHC)

What’s offered at ARHC

  • Behavioral health initial assessment
  • Short-term talk therapy
  • Medication management (psychiatrist)
  • Substance abuse screening + referrals
  • Family readiness / deployment-related counseling

Who can access

  • Active Duty Soldiers: priority access
  • Family members: space-available at ARHC; more commonly referred off-post through ATLAS
  • DA Civilians: limited; referred off-post

How to access

  • Active Duty: self-refer through the ARHC appointment line or walk in
  • Family / civilian: PCM referral → ARHC behavioral health booking

What ARHC isn’t great for

  • Long-term therapy (months of weekly sessions): referred to ATLAS
  • Specialty treatment (DBT, EMDR, trauma-focused CBT): often ATLAS
  • Autism / developmental specialty: ATLAS or EFMP coordination
  • Couples/family therapy: limited; ATLAS referral typical

Off-post via ATLAS (the longer-term path)

ATLAS directory

TRICARE Overseas’s ATLAS network includes many English-speaking psychologists + psychiatrists in Wiesbaden and Frankfurt. Full directory via International SOS.

Process

  1. ARHC PCM writes referral in ATLAS
  2. TRICARE Overseas approves (2-5 days)
  3. You book with specific provider
  4. Regular sessions; TRICARE bills directly

Wait times

  • Wiesbaden-area English therapists: 2-6 weeks for initial intake
  • Psychiatrist (medication): 4-8 weeks
  • Specialty (trauma, CBT, etc.): 4-12 weeks
  • Frankfurt providers: often shorter waits, 40-min train ride

Cost

TRICARE Overseas covers most of the visit; small co-pays depending on plan.

Chaplain (100% confidential pastoral care)

What makes chaplains different

Chaplains have absolute privileged confidentiality in conversations; cannot be compelled by command, court, or medical boards to disclose what you tell them. Behavioral health providers have limits on confidentiality (duty to warn, duty to report abuse, fitness-for-duty). Chaplains don’t.

When to use a chaplain

  • You want to talk without command or medical record involvement
  • You’re processing something spiritual / existential
  • You’re thinking through big life decisions (career, family, marriage)
  • You want a human ear without the medical-system overhead

How to access

  • Walk into the chaplain’s office at Clay Kaserne
  • Call the RSO main line and request a chaplain visit
  • No appointment required for initial conversation; ongoing meetings scheduled

What chaplains aren’t

  • Not clinical therapists; no meds, no diagnoses, no DSM-V
  • Not substitutes for deep trauma therapy
  • Not unlimited; they refer out for clinical needs

Family-specific

Kids + teens

  • School-based counselor (DoDEA); first stop for school-age kids
  • ARHC pediatric behavioral health; for diagnoses and medication
  • Civilian child psychologist via ATLAS; for therapy-heavy cases

Couples / relationship

  • Chaplain (pastoral counseling); free, confidential, ideal first step
  • ATLAS couples therapy (English-speaking); available; wait 4-8 weeks
  • Marriage-enrichment retreats; organized through ACS periodically

Spouse stress / spouse employment stress

  • ACS Employment Readiness Program (for career-related stress)
  • ACS spouse support groups; Family Readiness, FRG events
  • Military Family Life Consultant (MFLC); free, confidential, short-term

Deployment / separation

  • ACS Deployment Support; practical resources + emotional
  • FRG (Family Readiness Group); peer support
  • SYSSM (Spouse of Service Member); online community

Substance abuse

On-post

  • Army Substance Abuse Program (ASAP); assessments, treatment planning
  • Active Duty referral via command + behavioral health
  • Family referral via ARHC

Civilian

  • German treatment options available through ATLAS
  • AA / NA meetings; English-speaking meetings in Wiesbaden; check CAA.de or military Facebook groups

Specific populations

LGBTQ+

  • TRICARE covers standard mental health regardless of orientation
  • SHARP / EO for discrimination concerns
  • ATLAS includes LGBTQ-affirming providers; ask explicitly

PTSD / combat stress

  • Vet Center Europe (Kaiserslautern); Veterans Affairs; 90-min drive but worth it
  • ARHC combat stress services; short-term
  • ATLAS specialty PTSD therapists; include EMDR, CPT, Prolonged Exposure practitioners

Post-partum

  • Hebamme (midwife) often catches post-partum depression signs
  • ARHC behavioral health for evaluation + medication
  • ATLAS OB + psychiatrist collaboration for med management while breastfeeding

Hotlines + resources (save these in your phone)

  • 988; Veterans / Military Crisis Line
  • +49-162-297-5625; Domestic Violence Response
  • 53-SHARP (74277) or +49-162-296-6741; SHARP Victim Advocate
  • +49-162-296-6741; SHARP (24/7)
  • +49-911-398-2451; English-speaking Poison Control
  • 112; German emergency medical
  • ARHC appointment line; DSN 590-5762 / +49-6371-9464-5762
  • ACS main; DSN 548-9201 / +49-611-143-548-9201

Stigma considerations

Active Duty

  • Behavioral health visits do NOT automatically trigger security clearance review (most don’t)
  • Seeking help reflects self-awareness, not weakness
  • Chain of command does NOT see your BH medical records
  • Chaplain visits are not recorded in any duty file

DA Civilian

  • FEHB covers behavioral health; network differs from TRICARE
  • Employee Assistance Program (EAP) offers free short-term counseling

Family member

  • TRICARE Overseas treats BH like any other medical need
  • No career impact for dependents

Gotchas

  • Don’t wait until crisis. Waits for therapy are 4-8 weeks; start 3 months before you think you need it.
  • Language matters: ask your therapist if they’re comfortable in English before committing
  • Some German-specific treatments differ from US norms (e.g., schema therapy is more common in Germany)
  • Medication availability: some US-common meds aren’t in Germany; discuss substitutions with ATLAS psychiatrist
  • Telehealth from US-based TRICARE providers: sometimes allowed, rules varying
  • Deployment-related BH: goes through unit BH first, not ARHC civilian-style referrals
  • Don’t assume chaplain ≠ mental health support. Many Soldiers get what they need from pastoral conversation.
  • Check the MFLC (Military Family Life Consultant); 12 free short-term sessions per issue, no medical record

Crisis signals in yourself or others

Seek immediate help if you or someone you know:

  • Talks about wanting to die or self-harm
  • Withdraws from loved ones / responsibilities
  • Can’t sleep or sleeps way too much
  • Significant weight change, apathy
  • Feels hopeless or burdensome
  • Gives away treasured possessions
  • Shows recent intoxication / substance use escalation

Call 988. Text 988. Don’t wait. Don’t assume it’ll pass.
