Housing Before You Move

Housing areas, compared

Five on-post housing areas plus off-post. Here's what each actually gets you: building type, commute, kid-friendliness, parking reality.

Last verified 2026-04-16 3 min read

On this page▾
  1. What
  2. On-post housing areas
  3. Off-post; the RPP reality
  4. Gotchas
  5. Contact
  6. Source notes

What

You don’t pick your housing area; Housing Services does based on availability, rank, and family size. But knowing what each is like helps you rank your preferences on the form, and helps you decide between on-post and off-post if you have that choice.

On-post housing areas

Clay Kaserne (attached)

Small footprint of senior-officer and senior-NCO housing directly on the installation. Walking distance to work for Clay tenants. You won’t be offered this unless you’re O-5+ or E-9; most accompanied families are routed elsewhere.

Hainerberg Housing

The closest thing to “main-street America” on post. Townhouse-style stairwells, ACS and schools (Hainerberg Elementary, Wiesbaden Middle School) within walking distance, commissary + PX next door. Older buildings; floors creak, heating is quirky. Best fit for: families with elementary/middle-age kids who don’t want a car-dependent setup.

Aukamm Housing

Newer construction, quiet residential feel, hilltop location. Aukamm Elementary on-site. More parking and more green space than Hainerberg. 5-10 minute drive to Clay Kaserne. Best fit for: families who want modern finishes and don’t mind the commute.

Newman Village

The newest housing. Single-family homes and townhouses, built in the 2010s. Biggest kitchens, highest ceilings, best insulation. Farther from Clay Kaserne (10-15 min drive). Playground-heavy. Best fit for: larger families with young kids who want the space and don’t mind the drive.

Crestview Housing

Between Hainerberg and Aukamm geographically. Mix of mid-rise apartments and townhouses. Older stock but renovated. Close to Hainerberg schools. Best fit for: mid-rank families who don’t get Newman Village but want something more modern than 1960s Hainerberg.

Off-post; the RPP reality

The Rental Partnership Program (RPP) has a vetted list of German landlords who lease to SOFA-status personnel on approved terms. If HSO offers off-post as an option, here’s the trade-off:

Pros: You choose the neighborhood. More character, better grocery access (Rewe/Aldi/Edeka vs. commissary-only on-post). Often more space for the OHA money. Better integration into German life.

Cons: You set up utilities (gas, water, internet) yourself; and it’s in German. Rundfunkbeitrag (German TV tax, ~€18.36/month) applies. You deal with Nebenkosten (additional utility costs settled annually) which can surprise you. Commute to post adds 15-45 minutes depending on where you live.

Popular off-post neighborhoods

  • Wiesbaden proper (Rheingauviertel, Westend, Bierstadt): walkable, close to downtown, rents high
  • Taunusstein and Bad Schwalbach: smaller towns 20-30 min north, more house for the money
  • Mainz-Kastel: across the river, lower rents, still in the commute zone
  • Erbenheim: literally adjacent to Clay Kaserne’s back gate; closest off-post option, popular with junior NCOs

Gotchas

  • On-post housing rarely accommodates more than 4 bedrooms. Families with 4+ kids or mixed teen/young siblings often end up off-post by necessity.
  • Pets narrow your on-post options sharply. Some buildings are pet-restricted; bring up pets at your first HSO appointment.
  • Every lease HSO reviews is on the RPP list, but not every German landlord is on the RPP list. If you find a dream apartment independently, HSO will still vet the lease; but the landlord may not want to play by RPP rules, which kills the deal.
  • OHA is a reimbursement, not an advance. You pay the German landlord; the government reimburses monthly. First month’s rent + deposit comes out of your pocket.
  • Aukamm hilltop wind is real. Bring rain gear in winter.

Contact

See the Housing Services Office entry for the POC. The specific housing counselor you’re assigned to depends on the area you end up placed in; the main HSO number routes you.

Source notes

The housing-area profiles draw from the garrison’s individual area PDFs (Crestview and Newman Village have formal welcome PDFs; Hainerberg and Aukamm have less-current material) plus MilitaryOneSource’s government-housing overview. Off-post neighborhood observations are community knowledge, not policy; verify with HSO for current RPP inventory.
