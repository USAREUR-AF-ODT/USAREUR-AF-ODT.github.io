Patriot Express and flying to Europe

AMC's Patriot Express connects US East Coast to Ramstein. BWI/Philadelphia/JFK → Ramstein in 7–8 hours. Space-available seats for dependents; mandatory for some orders. Plan on pets and kids friendlier than commercial, but schedule is rigid.

What

Patriot Express is Air Mobility Command’s (AMC) rotator service between US East Coast airports and Ramstein Air Base, Germany. It’s how many military families fly to Europe on orders. It’s not an airline; it’s a DoD operation using commercial wide-body aircraft under contract.

For Wiesbaden arrivals, Ramstein is the entry point. From there, a 90-minute ground shuttle or rental car gets you to Wiesbaden.

When you’re on the Patriot Express

Your PCS orders specify whether you fly Patriot Express (mandatory for some), commercial (common), or a mix. Reasons orders may dictate Patriot Express:

  • Pets: Patriot Express is more pet-friendly than most US-to-Europe commercial options
  • Unaccompanied baggage with you: you’re carrying UB items that would exceed commercial allowances
  • Patriot Express itinerary on your orders: specifically directed route

Commercial flights (United, Delta, Lufthansa, etc.) to Frankfurt (FRA) are the alternative; shorter transit to Wiesbaden, more frequent schedule, often comparable cost once government-funded.

Patriot Express routes

Out of: BWI (Baltimore), Philadelphia, sometimes JFK (New York); routes change based on AMC tasking

Into: Ramstein AB, Germany (primary European entry)

Flight duration: 7-8 hours direct. 747, 767, or A330 aircraft depending on the day.

Booking and check-in

  1. Your orders specify whether you’re space-required (mandatory seat) or space-available
  2. Your losing unit or a PCS coordinator books Patriot Express tickets through AMC portal
  3. Check-in 3 hours before departure at BWI Cargo or Philly AMC terminal; not the commercial terminals
  4. Checked baggage: 2 bags per Soldier/dependent, each up to 70 lbs free, plus carry-on; excess baggage charges for oversize/overweight
  5. Pets: in-cabin (small) or cargo-PetSafe-style (larger) arrangements; coordinate through your losing LRC

On arrival at Ramstein

  1. Passport control: present US passport + SOFA orders. Quick process for military.
  2. Baggage claim: as normal
  3. Pet customs: at Ramstein, pets check through German Customs with the health certificate. Same process as Frankfurt commercial.
  4. Ground transport to Wiesbaden: 90 minutes north. Options:
    • AAFES rental car at Ramstein; pre-reserve, they hold for arrivals
    • Wiesbaden sponsor pickup; if sponsor is willing to drive 90 min each way
    • Military shuttle; sometimes scheduled; check via your PCS coordinator
    • Train from Kaiserslautern Hbf (Ramstein’s closest train station); 1.5-2 hours to Wiesbaden Hbf

Why Patriot Express is better (sometimes)

  • Pet-friendly: better than most US airlines; cargo PetSafe is standard, pets stored in climate-controlled holds
  • Familiar clientele: fellow military, so the check-in process moves predictably
  • No commercial surcharges: no extra baggage fees, checked pet fees, in-flight meal fees
  • Kids: relatively family-friendly; flight attendants trained for military families

Why Patriot Express is worse (sometimes)

  • Rigid schedule; delays cascade; if your pre-flight PCS timing is tight, the commercial option is more forgiving
  • Limited service; not daily; schedule gaps of 2-4 days are common
  • Eastern US only; if you’re coming from the West Coast, you route through BWI first
  • Comfort level; seats are tighter than some commercial premium classes
  • Space-available flights can get bumped; if space-A, don’t assume the flight is yours until you’re seated
  • Ramstein → Wiesbaden adds 90 min; FRA → Wiesbaden is 40 min. For some families this dwarfs the Patriot Express benefits

When to request commercial instead

Commercial flight to FRA is often cleaner for:

  • Pax-only PCS with no pets
  • Coming from the West Coast
  • Short-notice orders where flexibility matters
  • Traveling with elderly parents or high-maintenance kids who need commercial service standards
  • Tight PCS timing where Patriot Express schedule doesn’t fit

Alternatives to Patriot Express

  • Rhein-Main Air Base Gateway (historical name for Frankfurt-area flights, though RMAB no longer operates as USAF); effectively, Frankfurt commercial is now the alternative
  • United / Delta / Lufthansa direct to FRA; government-funded if on orders
  • Connection through London / Paris / Amsterdam; works but adds variables

Space-A travel (separate topic)

Space-A (Space-Available) is DIFFERENT from PCS Patriot Express travel. Space-A is a benefit for Active Duty, retirees, and their dependents to fly DoD military flights at very low cost during discretionary travel (leave, vacation).

  • Requires separate registration at the departure terminal
  • Priority tiers based on category (active duty leave, retirees, etc.)
  • Can take hours or days to actually board; no guaranteed schedule
  • Don’t plan space-A for time-critical travel
  • Useful for vacation flights; not useful for PCS

Pet logistics specifically

Patriot Express with pets

  • Crate must meet IATA standards
  • Pet age 8+ weeks, rabies vaccinated at least 30 days prior
  • EU Health Certificate with USDA endorsement (separate process; see Pet Import entry)
  • Space in cargo hold fills; book 4+ weeks ahead

Alternative: fly your pet cargo-only via pet shipping service

  • Companies like Across the Pond, PetRelocation; they charter cargo-only flights for pets
  • Pet arrives same day as you via separate transport
  • Cost: $2,000-$5,000+
  • Worth it for very large dogs, breed-sensitive pets, or tight-schedule cases

Gotchas

  • BWI Patriot Express terminal is NOT the commercial terminal. GPS to BWI will take you to the wrong place. Use the AMC terminal specifically.
  • Weather delays at Ramstein happen in winter; fog, ice. Build buffer into your plans.
  • Customs at Ramstein can be slow with pets; 20-60 min of paperwork per family. Plan accordingly.
  • No ground transport on-demand at Ramstein; rental car must be pre-reserved, military shuttle must be coordinated
  • If your Patriot Express gets cancelled and you’re already at the AMC terminal, AMC rebooks; but you lose a day
  • Return PCS via Patriot Express follows the same rules; book from Ramstein back to BWI/Philly months in advance
  • Space-A bumps happen. Bring a book. And snacks. And sleep aids.

Source notes

Synthesized from USAG Wiesbaden’s Getting Around garrison guidance and AMC’s standard Patriot Express operations. Specific routes and schedules change quarterly; always verify current operations through your gaining/losing PCS office before final booking.

  • Vehicle & POV Auto Skills Center: self-service bays, tools, and shop labor MWR-run garage on Clay Kaserne, BLDG 3500, open Tue–Sun. Rent lifts and bays by the hour, borrow tools, or pay shop labor ($89/hr) for oil changes, tire work, pre-PCS inspections, and diagnostics. Cheaper than German shops; great for DIY.
  • Vehicle & POV Public transport: RMV, Deutschlandticket, and the S-Bahn to Frankfurt Wiesbaden sits in the RMV transit network. The Deutschlandticket (~€58/month as of 2025) covers all regional and local transit nationwide. Buses + S1/S8/S9 S-Bahns and ICE trains from Hbf connect to Frankfurt (35 min) and Europe. No metro; buses and trains do everything.
  • Vehicle & POV Shipping your POV to Wiesbaden 4–8 weeks transit, one POV free per SOFA-status Soldier/DACiv, two for accompanied married. Drop it off at your losing VPC before you fly. Pick it up at the Bremerhaven / Emden VPC or have it delivered to Wiesbaden.
