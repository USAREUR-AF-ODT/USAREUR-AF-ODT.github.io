EFMP: enrollment, continuity, and what actually transfers

EFMP moves with you, but each installation maintains its own case file. Start the transfer the day orders drop. In Wiesbaden, ACS is your liaison; DoDEA handles school-side IEPs; TRICARE Overseas covers medical.

What

EFMP (Exceptional Family Member Program) documents a Family member’s medical, educational, or developmental needs and ties those needs to assignment decisions. In Wiesbaden, EFMP matters across three systems that don’t always talk to each other:

  • Medical EFMP; DoD-side, tied to TRICARE and the Army Health Clinic
  • Educational EFMP; DoDEA Wiesbaden (IEP / 504 / special ed)
  • Family-support EFMP; ACS local liaison, advocacy, respite care referrals

Enrollment is one process; continuity across a PCS is separate, and it’s the part most families underestimate.

Before the move; what to do CONUS-side

  1. Update your EFMP enrollment. If it’s been more than a year since the last medical/educational update, visit your losing station’s EFMP office and refresh. Outdated EFMP packets delay Wiesbaden’s assignment screening.
  2. Get a clearance letter. USAREUR assignments require EFMP medical screening. The Army Human Resources Command (HRC) EFMP branch coordinates; your losing EFMP office initiates. This can take 30-60 days; start early.
  3. Copy all IEPs, 504 plans, medical records. You want originals in one folder, copies in another. Email copies to yourself so you have cloud access.
  4. Get immunization records translated. DoDEA Wiesbaden and the Army Health Clinic both need English-language records. If your child was born or immunized at a non-US facility, translations take 2-4 weeks.
  5. Contact the Wiesbaden EFMP liaison at ACS (DSN 548-9201) 60-90 days before arrival. Introduce yourself; they’ll pre-stage your case file.

On arrival; what to register

  1. DEERS update; ensure your EFMP status flag is on for each enrolled dependent
  2. Wiesbaden Army Health Clinic; EFMP patient intake separate from routine patient registration; call the clinic directly and ask for the EFMP coordinator
  3. ACS EFMP liaison; in-person intro meeting within first 2 weeks
  4. DoDEA SLO; for school-age dependents with IEPs; SLO pre-coordinates with the receiving school
  5. DoDEA school psychologist; once registered at the Wiesbaden school, the school’s psychologist is your primary IEP point of contact

What transfers automatically

Almost nothing. EFMP data moves with you in the DoD personnel system, but case-specific coordination (therapy referrals, specialty providers, respite care hours) does not. You rebuild the support network at each installation.

The Wiesbaden Army Health Clinic handles primary care and referrals; specialists (speech therapy, OT, behavioral health) are mostly on the German civilian economy, coordinated through TRICARE Overseas referrals.

Specialty care in the Wiesbaden area

Wiesbaden’s medical catchment is well-equipped:

  • Asklepios Paulinen-Klinik; general pediatrics, pediatric rehab
  • Dr. Horst Schmidt Klinik (HSK); pediatric specialty clinics, neurology, developmental
  • Klinikum Mainz (Mainz University Clinic); complex pediatrics, rare conditions
  • Frankfurt UniKlinik; tertiary pediatric care, 45-min drive
  • Early Intervention Services (EIS); DoDEA-run for children birth to age 3 with developmental needs; co-located with ACS/CYS

English-speaking German specialists are easier to find in Wiesbaden than in smaller garrison cities (the international medical community is substantial). TRICARE Overseas handles billing directly with most.

DoDEA IEP transfer

  1. Send IEP copies to the Wiesbaden SLO 30 days before arrival
  2. On arrival, register the student with the receiving DoDEA school
  3. The school convenes a transfer IEP meeting within 10 school days
  4. Some accommodations transfer automatically; others require German-specific adjustments (facility access, curriculum alignment, language supports)

504 plans transfer similarly, with the school’s 504 coordinator.

Medical exemption for assignment

EFMP can flag an assignment as unsupportable if the medical/educational needs can’t be met locally. For Wiesbaden, the majority of common conditions are supported. High-complexity cases (rare-disease treatment centers, specialty transplant follow-up, etc.) may push the assignment elsewhere.

If your child’s condition is borderline, ask HRC EFMP branch for a case-by-case review before orders are cut. Getting reassigned after arrival is harder than preventing the assignment.

Gotchas

  • EFMP enrollment is not automatic. You must formally enroll; it’s not a DEERS default.
  • EFMP doesn’t share data with DoDEA. Sending an IEP to the medical side doesn’t put it in front of the school. Brief each system separately.
  • German specialists require ATLAS referrals through TRICARE Overseas. Plan 2-3 weeks for referral processing.
  • Respite care is limited. Wiesbaden ACS can refer, but the provider list is short. Get on the list early.
  • Summer PCS season compresses IEP meetings. If you’re arriving in July-August, the school psychologist is booked solid. Coordinate before arrival through SLO.
  • Developmental evaluations for birth-to-3 EIS are free through DoDEA but wait times run 6-10 weeks. Request evaluation in the first 2 weeks of arrival.
  • Not every specialty exists in Wiesbaden. Some families travel to Landstuhl (Army regional medical center, 90 min south) or Frankfurt for complex care.
  • EFMP enrollment does not negatively affect career. The myth that it tanks assignments is outdated; it tanks unsuitable assignments, which is the point.

Source notes

Process synthesized from MyArmyBenefits EFMP page, MilitaryOneSource Wiesbaden EFMP deep pages (4 separate sub-pages on preparing, health care, education directory, and EIS), and the Army Europe policy on EFMP continuity. Local specialist lists change frequently; confirm current providers with the Wiesbaden EFMP liaison at ACS.
