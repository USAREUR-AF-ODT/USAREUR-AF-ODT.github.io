Briefing

The first-month checklist

Week 1: sign-in, HSO, Finance, ID. Week 2: housing, SOFA stamp, TRICARE, banking. Week 3: Anmeldung, car registration, first LES. Week 4: schools settled, social integration. Print this.

What

A calendar-ordered master checklist of everything you need to do in the first 30 days. Sourced from the formal in-processing procedures and 20+ community experiences; items removed if they’re not universally applicable.

Print this. Check things off. Come back to the linked entries for detail.

Week 1; arrival + triage

Day 1 (arrival day)

  • Fly in, land at FRA or Ramstein
  • Rental car pickup OR sponsor ride
  • Check in to Wiesbaden Army Lodge on Hainerberg → start TLA clock (see Wiesbaden Lodge)
  • Get groceries at Hainerberg commissary OR emergency groceries from a local store
  • Sleep

→ See: First 72 Hours

Day 2

  • Sign in at your unit; mandatory for TLA clock continuation
  • ID Card Center (CAC renewal for you + first-card IDs for family); IDCO appointment
  • HSO first appointment; if Mon/Wed/Fri walk-in; otherwise appointment Tue/Thu

→ See: CAC, DBIDS, ID Card, HSO

Day 3

  • Finance office; PCS in-processing brief, TLA paperwork, travel voucher setup
  • DES / RMV; if POV shipped and you’re registering locally; otherwise wait for car to arrive
  • Wiesbaden Army Health Clinic; contact appointment line, register dependents

→ See: Vehicle Registration, First LES

Day 4-5

  • Newcomer orientation (ACS); first or third Tuesday of month
  • TRICARE Overseas registration; call International SOS, update address
  • DEERS verification; ensure dependents are current
  • Sponsor check-in; walk-through of Clay Kaserne with your sponsor

→ See: TRICARE Overseas, Army Community Service

Day 6-7 (weekend)

  • Wiesbaden downtown exploration; walk the Kurhaus area, hit a German bakery
  • File travel voucher via DTS; within 5 business days of arrival
  • Initial budget planning; rough sketch of OHA cap vs. rent, COLA allocation

Week 2; housing + medical + banking

Day 8-10

  • HSO housing appointment; counseling session, discuss on-post vs. off-post
  • View 2-3 properties (off-post) OR confirm on-post quarters assignment
  • Start Wiesbaden Army Health Clinic enrollment; first PCM appointment
  • First LES arrives; reconcile

Day 11-14

  • German bank account opened; Sparkasse, N26, or Revolut
  • US bank updated with APO address
  • AAFES Ration Card at ID Card Center (for Shoppette gas)
  • Passport/Visa Office; SOFA stamp started (must be completed by Day 90)
  • Start language basics; a few key German phrases (see German Survival)

→ See: Banking Options, SOFA Card

If you have pets

  • Wiesbaden VTF; register pet within first 30 days

→ See: Wiesbaden VTF

If you have kids

  • SLO contact; school enrollment coordination
  • DoDEA registration; your assigned school
  • CYS registration; WebTrac account, medical forms
  • Shot records translated if German-origin

→ See: School Liaison Office, DoDEA Schools, CYS

Week 3; residency + integration

Day 15-20

  • Lease signed (if off-post); HSO reviewed, OHA eligibility confirmed
  • Move-in day; coordinate with landlord for final meter readings
  • Utilities activated; gas, electric, water, internet
  • Anmeldung appointment (if off-post); Rathaus within 14 days of move-in
  • Wohnungsgeberbestätigung from landlord; required for Anmeldung

→ See: Anmeldung

Day 21

  • Rundfunkbeitrag auto-starts post-Anmeldung; set up direct debit
  • Vehicle registration at DES if POV arrived
  • First OHA LES; if lease approved, OHA starts flowing
  • TLA stops once moved into permanent quarters

Day 22-28

  • Start German class at VHS Wiesbaden (optional but valuable)
  • VAT office briefing; VAT forms for tax-free purchases
  • Social integration: meet neighbors, spouse groups, unit Family Readiness Group (FRG)
  • First weekend trip; Rüdesheim, Mainz, or local

→ See: VAT Forms, Weekend Trips

Week 4; settling and optimization

Day 29-30

  • All LES items flowing correctly; OHA, COLA, base pay
  • Travel voucher processed and deposited
  • Kids enrolled in school
  • Pet registered at VTF
  • Vehicle fully registered + inspected
  • Decide on German SIM (prepaid vs. contract)
  • Dental: enroll in TDP if family members need dental
  • Will + POA review at Legal Office (if life circumstances changed)

→ See: Legal Office

First 60 days; extended items

  • SOFA stamp completed in passport (by Day 90)
  • Driver’s license track decided (USAREUR cert vs. Führerschein)
  • Mid-tour savings plan updated (TSP adjustments)
  • VAT forms used aggressively for household setup purchases
  • First long-weekend travel (Paris, Amsterdam, Prague)
  • Enrolled in MWR program (gym, library, sports, SKIES Unlimited)

First 90 days; full settle

  • SOFA stamp in passport
  • Führerschein conversion started (if pursuing off-post license)
  • Rhythmic monthly budget / LES pattern established
  • Social community solidifying
  • Kids’ school routines + extracurriculars
  • Mid-tour orders updates (if applicable)

What if you miss something?

Missed TLA extension window

Contact Finance immediately. Sometimes retroactive extensions approved with good documentation.

Missed Anmeldung window (>14 days)

File anyway. Fines theoretical; practical outcome usually a polite warning.

Missed SOFA stamp (>90 days)

Contact Passport/Visa Office. Retroactive processing with explanation. Avoid traveling until resolved.

POV not yet arrived, DES closing

DES handles delayed registrations routinely. Priority call the office when POV arrives.

Tools that help

  • Printable version of this list; use the print function on this page
  • Calendar app; block the appointments mentioned above 2-3 days before each
  • Paper notebook; write down every person’s name, DSN, and notes
  • Google Maps offline maps; download Wiesbaden area before you arrive

Source notes

Checklist synthesized from the OIG Inprocessing Brief, the garrison in-processing page, and MilitaryOneSource’s check-in procedures for Wiesbaden. Specific appointment requirements and paperwork vary by unit; always verify unit-specific requirements with your gaining S1.
