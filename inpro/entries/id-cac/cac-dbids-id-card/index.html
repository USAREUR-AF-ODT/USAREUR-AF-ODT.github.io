CAC, DBIDS, and the ID Card Center

Your CAC comes with you from CONUS. Family members re-badge at the ID Card Center: CAC for teens 14+, DD-1173 for dependents under 14. DBIDS (installation access) is separate, automatic once enrolled.

Last verified 2026-04-16 4 min read

What

Three overlapping ID systems for SOFA-status personnel and dependents in Wiesbaden:

  1. CAC (Common Access Card); your federal employee / Soldier ID, carries PKI certificates for DoD systems
  2. DD-1173 / USID (Uniformed Services ID); dependent ID for family members under 14 (DD-1173) and 75+ (older-retiree card)
  3. DBIDS (Defense Biometric Identification System); the physical-access ID check when you enter any installation

The ID Card Center issues CACs and DD-1173s. DBIDS enrollment happens at the same office (or at any installation gate with DBIDS kiosks). All three are things you handle during your first two weeks.

Day-1 priorities

  • Soldier/DA Civ: your CAC from CONUS is valid. If it expires within 90 days of arrival, reissue immediately; the Wiesbaden ID Card Center will renew.
  • Family members 10+: all dependents need updated IDs reflecting Wiesbaden as the duty station (old duty station printed on the card causes confusion at SOFA-stamped border crossings).
  • Teens turning 14: first DD-1173 becomes a YA-1173 (young adult card). Process it within 30 days of the 14th birthday; the old card goes invalid.
  • Spouses: replacement ID may be needed if marriage happened after the original card or if the card is damaged/expired.

How to get in

Book on IDCO: idco.dmdc.osd.mil; appointments 2-8 weeks out. Walk-ins are theoretically accepted but realistically a 2-4 hour wait during PCS peak (May-August).

Best time to book: early morning, Monday or Wednesday. Avoid the days after federal holidays.

Documents checklist

For CAC reissue (you, the Soldier/DA Civ)

  • Current expiring CAC
  • Passport or state-issued photo ID as secondary
  • Orders or SF-50

For DD-1173 / USID (dependents 10+)

  • Dependent’s existing ID (if any)
  • Dependent’s birth certificate or passport (must bring one or the other)
  • Two forms of secondary ID (for 16+: school ID + passport; for 10-15: school ID + shot record or similar)
  • Sponsor must be physically present OR have a notarized Power of Attorney (DD Form 1172-2)

For spouses (ID renewal)

  • Old ID
  • Marriage certificate (certified copy)
  • Passport
  • Sponsor in person OR POA

For new spouses (first card)

  • Marriage certificate (apostilled if issued outside US)
  • Passport of spouse
  • Sponsor in person (no POA allowed for first issuance)
  • DEERS enrollment confirmation (happens first through S1 / HR, then ID card)

DEERS vs DD-1173; the order matters

DEERS (the dependent database) must be current before the ID Card Center can print a card. Update DEERS first through your unit S1 / civilian HR office, then book ID Card Center for the physical card.

Mistakes in DEERS (wrong middle name, wrong SSN) have to be fixed there before the ID Card Center can help. Plan a DEERS update appointment before your ID Card appointment.

DBIDS

DBIDS is the physical-access credential; the code that lets you through installation gates. Enroll once; it’s tied to your CAC or DD-1173 electronically. In Wiesbaden, DBIDS enrollment happens:

  • At the ID Card Center during your first CAC/ID appointment (automatic)
  • At the Clay Kaserne visitor control center (for contractors and visitors who don’t hold a CAC)

Visitor DBIDS (for non-SOFA guests visiting you); they need to be sponsored through the Visitor Control Center. Bring the visitor’s passport copy 1-2 weeks before the visit.

Common lost/stolen scenarios

Per USAG Wiesbaden CPL 19 (lost/stolen ID policy):

  1. Report the loss to the MP immediately; they issue an MP report number
  2. Bring the MP report number + photo ID to the ID Card Center for replacement
  3. Replacement fees apply for repeated losses (first one usually free)
  4. If the card was stolen with other identification, file a broader identity theft report

Gotchas

  • “Walk-in” is advertised but rarely fast. Always use IDCO appointments.
  • Kids under 10 don’t need a card; they use the sponsor’s card for medical access. Card requirement kicks in at 10.
  • Teens turn 14 = new card cycle. The 14th birthday triggers re-issue. Same at 18 and 21.
  • Card renewals close to PCS-out can be coordinated to align expiry with departure. Don’t renew two months before PCS if you can help it.
  • Social Security Number must match DEERS exactly; a typo on DEERS creates a headache that no ID card appointment can resolve.
  • Expired US passport with a SOFA stamp is invalid for re-entry to Germany if you leave. Renew the US passport separately before the SOFA stamp renewal (see Legal / SOFA Card entry).
  • CAC PINs get forgotten. PIN reset requires in-person visit to the ID Card Center. Budget an appointment just for this if it happens.
  • Dual-citizen dependents have extra paperwork; bring the US passport, not just the EU one.

Source notes

Process synthesized from the USAG Wiesbaden in-processing page, DMDC’s milConnect dependents FAQ, and the USAG Wiesbaden CPL 19 policy for lost/stolen IDs. Hours and appointment systems change; confirm via idco.dmdc.osd.mil before showing up.
