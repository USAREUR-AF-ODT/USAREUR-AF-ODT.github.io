Garrison directorates: who to call for what

USAG Wiesbaden runs through ~15 directorates (DES for security, DPW for housing maintenance, DHR for personnel, DFMWR for family programs, Legal, SHARP, etc.). Garrison HQ at BLDG 3340 North Clay. This entry maps the problem to the office.

What

Tenant units fight and train; the garrison runs the installation. Different chains of command. Your unit won’t fix a broken boiler in your on-post quarters; DPW will. Your unit won’t issue your ID card; DHR will. Knowing which directorate owns which problem saves days during in-processing and whenever something breaks later.

USAG Wiesbaden (the garrison) falls under Installation Management Command (IMCOM); Europe. The Garrison Commander is an O-6 (or senior O-5). Below the commander, work splits across directorates, each led by a director (often GS-14/15 civilian) with a mix of military and civilian staff.

Where

Garrison Headquarters: BLDG 3340, North Clay Kaserne. Most directorates have offices within walking distance of HQ on Clay; some (DPW, DES substations) have outposts on Hainerberg and Mainz-Kastel.

When

Garrison HQ and most directorates: Mon-Fri 0730-1630. DES (police, fire, emergency services) runs 24/7. Most service-desk directorates (DHR/MPD, Legal, ACS) close at 1600 or 1630 and do not take walk-ins in the last half hour.

Directorates; what they do and when you need them

Directorate of Emergency Services (DES)

Military Police, physical security, access control, gate operations, fire and emergency services. Call DES for: lockouts at your on-post quarters, lost military ID recovery, vehicle registration/decal issues, force-protection questions, domestic disturbance, emergencies (911 on post; follow German 110/112 off post and then notify DES).

Directorate of Public Works (DPW)

Buildings, utilities, infrastructure, housing maintenance, environment. Call DPW for: water leaks in on-post quarters, heating failures, electrical issues, tree damage, road conditions, work orders. The housing maintenance line is run through DPW.

Directorate of Human Resources (DHR)

Umbrella for Military Personnel Division (MPD); ID cards, DEERS, records, promotions paperwork; and Army Continuing Education Services (ACES), Administrative Services, and the Army Substance Abuse Program (ASAP). Call DHR/MPD for: new ID cards, DEERS updates, re-enlistment in-processing, education benefits, substance-abuse counseling/referrals.

Directorate of Family and Morale, Welfare and Recreation (DFMWR)

Runs MWR programs: commissary support coordination, Army Community Service (ACS), Child and Youth Services (CYS), fitness centers, outdoor rec, library, food and beverage outlets. Call DFMWR for: program registration, facility rental, CYS/CDC waitlists, ACS classes and services, spouse-employment workshops.

Directorate of Plans, Training, Mobilization & Security (DPTMS)

Installation-level training coordination, range scheduling, mobilization support, emergency operations. Most Family members never interact with DPTMS directly; it’s the directorate your unit S3 and mobilizing Reservists talk to.

Civilian Personnel Advisory Center (CPAC)

HR for DoW Civilians and NAF employees. Call CPAC for: civilian hiring questions, in-processing for NAF/GS positions, civilian benefits, performance issues, spouse federal employment (TA).

Legal Office

USAG Wiesbaden SJA. Call Legal for: free legal assistance (wills, POAs, notary, family-law consultation, consumer disputes), tax assistance during tax season, SOFA questions, claims (household goods damage, lost luggage).

Religious Support Office (RSO)

Chaplain services, chapel coordination, faith-community referrals. Call RSO for: counseling under chaplain privilege, marriage/baptism/memorial coordination, religious-accommodation requests, small-group connection. See Chapel Services for service schedule.

Logistics Readiness Center (LRC)

Theater-level logistics and household-goods support. Call LRC for: HHG shipment tracking, claims for shipping damage, vehicle processing, customs questions, transportation office appointments.

Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) and Military Equal Opportunity (MEO)

EEO for civilian discrimination complaints; MEO for military. Call if you experience or witness discrimination, harassment, or a hostile work environment and need a formal complaint pathway.

Sexual Harassment/Assault Response and Prevention (SHARP)

Restricted and unrestricted reporting for sexual harassment and assault. 24/7 hotline. Separate from chain of command.

Safety Office

Installation safety, accident reporting, safety training, hazard reports. Most interactions are through your unit safety NCO, but civilians report directly.

Public Affairs Office (PAO)

Media inquiries, community announcements, garrison social media. Not a “complaint office”; PAO handles external communication, not personal disputes.

Resource Management Office (RMO) and PAIO

Budget, manpower analysis, performance measurement. Internal garrison staff functions; Families rarely contact directly.

Home-Based Businesses (HBB) Office

Licensing and permissions for operating a business from on-post quarters (tutoring, childcare, crafts). Required before you accept money for a service from your home.

Escalation paths

  • Minor issues (slow maintenance, confusing process); work through the directorate’s front desk, then ask for the division chief.
  • Persistent issues; Interactive Customer Evaluation (ICE) at the USAG Wiesbaden portal. Every submission is tracked; managers read them.
  • Command-level concerns; Commander’s Hotline on the garrison website. Goes straight to the Garrison Commander’s office.
  • Crisis; DES 24/7 line, or German 110 (police) / 112 (ambulance/fire) with a post-incident notification to DES.
  • Specialized chains; SHARP, EEO, Inspector General, and Chaplain all bypass normal chain of command. Use them when appropriate.

Gotchas

  • Your unit ≠ the garrison. If you call your S1 about a housing boiler, you’ve wasted everyone’s time. Route to DPW.
  • Most directorates close 1600-1630. Last walk-in typically accepted 30 minutes before close. Plan around that.
  • Not every directorate has a Hainerberg outpost. Most of your paperwork traffic is on Clay.
  • ICE is not a venting outlet. Specific, actionable feedback gets action; rants get filed. Submit one issue at a time.
  • ID Card Center (DHR/MPD) is appointment-driven, not walk-in. Book through the USFJ/IDCO appointment portal weeks ahead.
  • Fire, MP, and medical emergencies; on post, call 911; off post, call German 110 (police) or 112 (medical/fire). DES will coordinate follow-up if applicable.
  • The Commander’s Hotline is for real issues. Misuse (routine complaints that belong in ICE) clogs the channel.
