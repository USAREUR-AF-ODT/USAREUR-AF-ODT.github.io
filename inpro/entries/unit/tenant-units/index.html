USAG Wiesbaden tenant units: who's where

V Corps HQ, 2nd Theater Signal Brigade, 66th Military Intelligence Brigade, 5th Signal Command, and a dozen more. Clay Kaserne is the main footprint; many tenants have outposts at Hainerberg and Mainz-Kastel.

What

USAG Wiesbaden is a garrison, not a division. It hosts dozens of tenant units across multiple Kasernen. Understanding who lives where helps with: finding your unit during in-processing, knowing where to go for formations, understanding the command relationships that matter.

Most tenant units are on Clay Kaserne (the garrison’s main installation, on the west side of Wiesbaden at Flugplatz Erbenheim). Others are split across Hainerberg, Mainz-Kastel, and the Amelia Earhart Center.

Major tenant units

Command-level

  • U.S. Army Europe and Africa (USAREUR-AF); HQ element at Clay Kaserne; the 4-star command that oversees all Army forces in Europe and Africa
  • V Corps (Forward); forward element of V Corps (HQ is at Fort Knox, KY); command element coordinating rotational forces in Europe
  • 5th Signal Command (Theater); Army theater-level signal command; Clay Kaserne is HQ, with units across Europe
  • 56th Theater Multi Domain Command; newer multi-domain operations HQ

Intelligence

  • 66th Military Intelligence Brigade; INSCOM’s theater brigade for Europe. HQ at Clay Kaserne.
    • 2nd Military Intelligence Battalion
    • 24th Military Intelligence Battalion
  • 522nd Military Intelligence Battalion (207th MI Brigade); separate battalion at Clay Kaserne

Signal / Communications

  • 2nd Theater Signal Brigade; theater-wide Army communications brigade
    • 102nd Signal Battalion
  • Regional Cyber Center; Europe (RCC-E); cyber defense element (CAC users only for info)
  • 7th Weather Squadron; Air Force weather support to USAREUR

Aviation

  • 1st Battalion, 214th Aviation Regiment (12th Combat Aviation Brigade); General Support Aviation Battalion at Clay Kaserne

Other

  • 405th Army Field Support Battalion / LRC-Wiesbaden; logistics support
  • 485th Intelligence Squadron; Air Force element (Clay Kaserne)
  • Branch Movement Control Team; Wiesbaden (39th Transportation Battalion); movement control
  • Training Support Center Wiesbaden (7th Army Training Command); training support
  • U.S. Army Counterintelligence Europe (ACI-E) Wiesbaden Field Office
  • U.S. Army Customs Agency; Europe Wiesbaden Field Office
  • 529th Military Police Company
  • 4th Air Support Operations Group; Air Force element
  • 209th Digital Liaison Detachment
  • Company C, 457th Civil Affairs Company

Garrison and support

  • USAG Wiesbaden HQ; garrison command element
  • DES (Directorate of Emergency Services)
  • DHR (Directorate of Human Resources)
  • DPW (Directorate of Public Works)
  • DFMWR (Directorate of Family, MWR); runs ACS, CYS, MWR facilities
  • LRC (Logistics Readiness Center)

Kasernen layout

Clay Kaserne

The main installation. V Corps (F), 66th MI, 2nd Sig, 5th Sig, USAREUR-AF HQ, USAG Wiesbaden HQ, most garrison support (Finance, DES, DHR, DPW), Army Health Clinic, Exchange, Lodge, Commissary.

Access: main gate on Wiesbaden Army Airfield side. CAC or DBIDS required.

Hainerberg Housing Area

Family housing, ACS (Bldg 7790), commissary, PX complex, elementary and middle schools, chapel. The non-work half of Wiesbaden Army for most Families.

Mainz-Kastel

Former station, now largely repurposed; some training and special elements. Limited current-day tenant presence.

Amelia Earhart Center

Named for Amelia Earhart (the aviator). Small footprint. Check with garrison for current use.

Typical arrival for a new Soldier

  1. Sign in at your gaining unit (wherever that is)
  2. In-process at the garrison; DES for registration, DHR for HR, Finance for pay, etc.
  3. Check with your unit’s S1 about unit-specific in-processing (uniforms, equipment issue, unit briefings)
  4. Clay Kaserne is where 80% of your first week happens

Gotchas

  • Your unit may have its own in-processing requirements above what the garrison does. Check with your gaining S1 early. Some units require pre-arrival read-ahead materials.
  • Clearances: if your unit is intel / cyber, clearance continuity matters. Coordinate with your losing station’s S2 and your gaining S2 60+ days before arrival. Clearances don’t auto-transfer; you may need a new investigation if it’s been a while.
  • V Corps Forward and the main V Corps (CONUS) have different administrative paths. If you’re V Corps, confirm which element you’re reporting to.
  • Some tenant units have outposts at Hainerberg or Mainz-Kastel. Don’t assume “Clay Kaserne” = everyone’s HQ. Confirm the exact duty location with your orders.
  • Inprocessing brief schedules rotate. OIG-level briefs happen quarterly; CCIU cyber briefings are regular. Check the garrison bulletin for the week’s schedule.
  • Unit families: sponsor assignment usually goes through the unit, not the garrison. Build a relationship with your unit’s Family Readiness Group (FRG); they’re often the fastest source of real-time help.

Source notes

Tenant unit list synthesized from the USAG Wiesbaden Tenant Units page and MilitaryOneSource’s major-units overview. Some smaller elements rotate through; the major commands (V Corps, 66th MI, 2nd Sig, 5th Sig, USAREUR-AF) are stable. Always confirm your specific unit’s location via your gaining S1 before finalizing travel plans.

Garrison HQ at BLDG 3340 North Clay. This entry maps the problem to the office.
  • Unit Integration Requesting a sponsor: how to make it happen before you arrive Sponsor assignment is mandatory under AR 600-8-8 but often slow. Email the gaining unit S1 90 days before RNLTD with your orders and family details. No reply in 2 weeks: escalate to the gaining unit's CSM or 1SG. Use Total Army Sponsorship Program (TASP) as a paper trail.
  • Unit Integration Sponsor gone dark: what to do when your assigned sponsor disappears A silent sponsor is common. Fallback: call the gaining unit S1 for reassignment, loop ACS Relocation Readiness for a volunteer replacement, lean on Lodge and ICE for week-one help. You can finish in-processing without a functioning sponsor.
  • Unit Integration Reporting for duty: your first week at the unit S1 handles in-processing, Operations owns the mission, Supply gears you up. Expect 3–5 days of admin before real work starts. Learn your sponsor chain, your ERB/SRP, and where to find the S1 lanyard-shaped office.
