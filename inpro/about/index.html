About

Not affiliated. Just a guide.

inpro is an unofficial, community-maintained PCS information portal for Wiesbaden, Germany. Run by people who've been through it, written for people about to.

What this is

89 hand-written entries covering what you need to know before, during, and after moving to Wiesbaden. Every page is synthesized from official sources, cross-checked against community experience, and cited.

What this isn't

Not affiliated with the US Army, the Department of War, DoD, USAG Wiesbaden, or any official organization. No ads. No account required. No tracking cookies. No PII collected. Works offline after first visit.

Not a policy document. For official policy, confirm with your unit S1, the relevant garrison office, or the cited source.

How content gets written

Every entry has a "Last verified" date and a Sources panel. We source-tier our references:

  • T1 Official: Army, DoD, TRICARE, DoDEA, USAG, MyArmyBenefits, specific PDFs hosted by garrison
  • T2 Semi-official: MilitaryOneSource, AFN, Stars & Stripes
  • T3 Community: Expat blogs, Reddit, verified US-military community groups — paraphrased, never quoted
  • T4 German-side: Stadt Wiesbaden, ADAC, Bürgeramt, German federal websites

An entry marked "Verify before acting" has information (usually POCs and hours) that rotates quickly. Call the office before driving.

Editorial approach

We write with opinions where opinions help (restaurant picks, neighborhood trade-offs, travel recommendations). We stick to facts where facts matter (DSN numbers, policy, deadlines). Each entry tells you which is which.

We do not republish other people's content. We read their content as research, then write our own synthesis. Closed Facebook groups are research material only — we don't quote members.

Found something wrong?

Every entry has a "Report outdated info" link at the bottom. Corrections welcome. Fast turnaround.

For routine corrections — outdated POCs, broken links, factual errors — the report link is the right path. We aim to triage within 72 hours and resolve within a week.

Takedowns & sensitive concerns

If something on this site needs to come down — your name or photo appearing where it shouldn't, an address that should be redacted, content that touches operational details — use the takedown channel, not the regular feedback link.

Send to inpro+takedown at the same alias used for feedback (the email is replied to from a project-only label, not a personal inbox). Include:

  • The full URL of the page
  • What needs to be removed or changed (be specific — quote the exact text or describe the image)
  • Why (one sentence is fine)
  • How to reach you for follow-up if needed

We aim to acknowledge takedown requests within 24 hours and act within 72 hours. If a request involves OPSEC or a credible safety concern, we take the page down first and discuss after. We do not publish names of takedown requesters.

This is a community project, not a legal entity. We can't accept formal legal process; if your matter requires that, contact USAG Wiesbaden Public Affairs.

How it's built

Static site, server-free, PWA-enabled. Code is MIT-licensed; content is CC-BY-SA 4.0. The public repo link will appear here once we ship.

Privacy

No accounts. No tracking cookies. Your profile selection (Soldier / DACiv / Family etc.) lives only in your browser's localStorage — we never see it. Aggregate pageview counts are collected via Cloudflare Web Analytics, with no cookies and no personal data.
